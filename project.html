Protect the Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon is one of Earth’s most vital ecosystems—home to millions of species, indigenous communities, and a massive carbon sink that stabilizes our climate.

Take action Learn why

Why the Amazon matters

Global climate regulation

The Amazon stores vast amounts of carbon and generates moisture that influences rainfall patterns across the continent, helping moderate global climate systems.

Unmatched biodiversity

Millions of species—from jaguars to orchids—depend on the Amazon’s complex habitats, many of which are found nowhere else on Earth.

Indigenous stewardship

Indigenous peoples have protected the forest for generations. Supporting their rights and knowledge is essential for conservation.

Freshwater and livelihoods

The river system sustains communities, fisheries, and agriculture across the region.

Key threats to the Amazon

Deforestation & illegal logging

Clearing for timber, cattle, and crops fragments habitats and drives species loss.

Fires & drought

Climate change and land clearing increase fire frequency, degrading forest resilience.

Mining & infrastructure

Pollution and roads open remote areas, accelerating habitat loss and conflict.
